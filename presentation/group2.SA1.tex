% ── SLIDE 7: CONCLUSIONS ────────────────────────────────────
\begin{frame}{Conclusions \& Clinical Insights}

\begin{columns}[T]

  \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
    \textbf{Statistical Conclusions}
    \vspace{3pt}
    \begin{itemize}
      \small
      \item \textbf{Generalised Gamma} is the best model
            (AIC = 8{,}212.9) among 7 candidates
      \item Flexible hazard shape outperforms Weibull,
            Exponential and Gamma
      \item $Q{<}0$ confirms a \textbf{non-monotone hazard}
            not captured by standard models
      \item High SD (1{,}221 days) reflects substantial
            \textbf{patient heterogeneity}
    \end{itemize}
  \end{column}

  \begin{column}{0.48\textwidth}
    \textbf{Clinical Implications}
    \vspace{3pt}
    \begin{itemize}
      \small
      \item \textbf{Critical window:} highest death risk
            in the first $\sim$300 days --- intensive
            post-surgical follow-up is essential
      \item \textbf{Mean survival 5.8 years} is a
            realistic planning benchmark
      \item $\sim$45\% survive beyond 3{,}000 days ---
            a favourable long-term subgroup exists
      \item \texttt{nodes} count should be explored
            in future covariate models
    \end{itemize}
  \end{column}

\end{columns}

\vspace{6pt}
\begin{center}
  \small\itshape
  The non-monotone hazard identified by the Generalized Gamma model supports a biphasic clinical strategy: intensive early monitoring during the first year post-surgery, followed by structured long-term survivorship care thereafter
\end{center}
